Instruments and Effects


Adding Instruments to Your Track

We have a simple drum loop going, now we want to add something else. For the sake of the tutorial, we will be using a simple piano sound. Follow these steps:

  1. In the top right corner, click Add to open up the drop down menu.
  2. Many of these listed items are instruments, but find the one that says FL Keys and click on it to add it to our Channel Rack.
  3. Once the FL Keys window pops up, you can click on the piano keys displayed to play a sound. Dont worry about all of the knobs for now. Those are beyond the scope of this tutorial.
  4. Go back to your channel rack. Notice how the numbers 1 through 4 are listed next to the sound names of our drums. Our piano sound doesn't have a number. Click on the dashes and drag down until the number 5 is in that box. This will come in handy for the next section.

Effects

What are Effects

In this context, effects are things that you can add to your track to change the way that things sounds. One example of an effect is called delay, which is like an echo. A very popular effect is called reverb, which gives a sound a feeling like is is in a building like a drum room or cathedral. Effects like filters and equalizers can alter the frequency of a sound.

Adding Effects

Remember earlier when we made sure to put the number 5 next to our piano sound? This is where we need that. We are going to add some effects to our piano through the Mixer Rack

  1. Open the Mixer Rack by pressing F9.
  2. Click on the number 5. This is where we routed out piano sound earlier.
  3. On the left, click on Slot 1 and select Fruity Delay 3. Click on one of the piano keys. Do you hear the echo? Click on the green light next to where we just added the delay effect to turn it off for now.
  4. Click on Slot 2 and then select Fruity Reeverb 2. On the interface that pops up, click on the right arrow where it says Presets. It should now say Cathedral in that same area. You may notice some of the knobs moved on the interface. Play a piano key again. Hear how it sounds like it is far away? Like on the other side of a big cathedral. We will leave the reverb effect on for now.
