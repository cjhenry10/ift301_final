Getting Started


Opening FL Studio

There is a lot happening here. Don't be too alarmed, though. Like most programs, many of the UI items are different ways of doing the same thing. We will go through the basics one step at a time.

The small window near the center of the page is called the Channel Rack. This is where we insert the sounds that we want to use. By default, there are drum sounds loaded into the channel rack. For this tutorial we will just stick with the basic drum sounds.

The number that currently reads "130.00" along the top of the screen is our beats per minute, or BPM. This is the speed of our song's rhythm. Higher BPM typically means a faster song.

These are the two sections we will look at on this page of the website.

Choosing a Genre

Artist Examples

You can make any genre of music with FL Studio. Some more popular genres are Electronic Dance Music(EDM), Hip Hop, and Pop.

Some EDM artists are Avicii, deadmau5, and Martin Garrix. Hip Hop artists are artists such as Dr. Dr, Kanye West, or Lex Luger. Some Pop examples are Coldplay, Ed Sheeran, and Taylor Swift.

BPM Table

Genre BPM

Dubstep 135-145 BPM

You are in no way restricted by the data in this table, however this is what these genre's typical BPM range is. We will choose the value of 128 because it is valid for both Trance and House music, both of which are subgenres of EDM.

Setting a BPM and Drum Loop

Now that we know a little more about what is happening, we can start getting into the fun stuff. Let's start creating the foundation of our song.

  1. Click on the BPM indicator that says "130" and drag the mouse down until it reads "128".
  2. On the channel rack, click the little arrow on the top left to turn on looping mode.
  3. Right click on the word "Kick" and click "Fill each 4 steps". This is the kick drum sound for our song.
  4. Right click on the word "Hat" and click "Fill each 2 steps". This is our hi hat sound.
  5. Click on the boxes of the first dark red squares in each group of 4 next to where it says "Clap". We are manually entering the clap sound into our loop.

Each which box on the channel rack represents a hit of our instrument. The channel rack will go from left to right and play a recording of that instrument every time it passes over one of those highlighted boxes.
